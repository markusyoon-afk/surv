🦉 SURV — Privacy

Last updated: July 2026

The short version

Your data lives on your device. SURV has no accounts, no passwords, and no tracking. We can't read your decisions because they never reach us.

What SURV stores, and where

  • On your device only: your name, your owl, your decisions (SURVs), votes, verdicts, and SAGE scores — kept in your browser's local storage. Deleting the app's website data erases everything.
  • Location (optional): if you tap "Use my location," your coordinates are sent to OpenStreetMap services (Nominatim/Overpass) to find nearby places, and are not stored by SURV beyond your device.
  • Entertainment charts: the app fetches public Apple iTunes charts to suggest real shows and movies. No personal data is sent.
  • Claude AI (optional, bring-your-own-key): if you connect an Anthropic API key, your question text is sent to Anthropic to generate options, under your own key and Anthropic's terms. Off by default.

Sharing

When you share a SURV or invite link, the decision text travels inside the link itself to whoever you send it to — the same as texting them. Nothing is posted anywhere public unless you choose the Forest.

No tracking

No analytics, no ads, no cookies beyond local storage, no fingerprinting, no third-party trackers.

Changes

If SURV adds a shared backend (accounts and a live network), this policy will be updated first and the change will be announced in the app.
